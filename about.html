About

I'm a postgraduate student at the University of Edinburgh. My programme is Evolution of Language & Cognition. I graduated from the University of York in 2016 with a degree in Linguistics and Mathematics. I was born and raised in the vicinity of Liverpool.

I blog at The House Carpenter and Tumblr at house-carpenter. In both places I post about whatever I'm interested in at the moment, which is most often something to do with linguistics or mathematics, which I guess are my two “fields”. Although I've never studied it at university, I have another fairly strong academic interest in anthropology. Non-academic interests include natural history and folk music.

This website is for anything I feel like making public which doesn't seem suitable to put on my blog or Tumblr, or which is too inconvenient to put into that format, such as really long essays (although don't take that as a guarantee that I'll be writing any of those), or mathematical notes which are a nightmare to get into the right format for WordPress (this is my real reason for making this website).
